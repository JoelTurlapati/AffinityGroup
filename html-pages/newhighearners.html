The Six Gaps That Cost
High Earners the Most

These are the most common wealth-building blind spots we see in $250k+ earners. Our role is to close every one of them — proactively, before they cost you more time and money.

Overpaying in Taxes Every Year

Without year-round proactive planning, the IRS captures far more than necessary. Every dollar overpaid is a dollar that never compounds over the next 20 years.

The 401(k) Ceiling

Maxing a 401(k) at $23,000/year while earning $300,000 leaves massive investment potential untapped. Most high earners have no clear strategy for the rest.

Scattered, Uncoordinated Accounts

Old 401(k)s from former employers, random brokerage accounts, and bank savings — all with no unified strategy. Coordination gaps can cost as much as the investments themselves.

No Time to Manage Any of It

Career, family, community — your bandwidth is maxed. Financial planning keeps getting delayed. We act as your dedicated financial team so decisions happen on your behalf, not on your to-do list.

College Planning Gap

High income phases out many education deductions, making 529 strategy and funding timelines critical. Without a plan, college costs arrive as a financial shock rather than a planned milestone.

No Clear Retirement Runway

Most high earners cannot answer: “When could I stop working if I wanted to?” We build a precise retirement timeline — so financial independence has a date, not just a dream.
