Business Owners

Tax Preparation

Individual Tax Preparation: Built Around the Full Financial Picture

Affinity Private Tax does not operate as a traditional tax-preparation firm serving the general public.

Individual tax preparation is available exclusively to Affinity Wealth Total Wealth Management clients and business owners who participate in our Outsourced CFO relationship.

Why This Matters

Many tax preparers only see your financial information once per year. Their work is often limited to the documents you provide during tax season, which can make it difficult to understand the complete picture of your finances, investment activity, business operations, and long-term planning goals.

At Affinity Private Tax, our team works from a more complete financial picture.

For Total Wealth Management clients, we maintain an ongoing understanding of your investment accounts, retirement planning, income sources, tax projections, charitable strategies, and major financial decisions throughout the year. This allows tax preparation to become part of a broader planning process rather than a once-a-year filing exercise.

For business owners participating in our Outsourced CFO service, we often have visibility into both the business financial records and the owner’s personal tax situation. This coordination allows us to better understand how business activity may impact the individual return and identify planning opportunities across both areas.

The Advantage of an Integrated Approach

Because tax preparation is connected to wealth management, tax planning, bookkeeping, and business advisory services, our team is able to:

  • Review tax returns within the context of your broader financial situation
  • Coordinate planning decisions throughout the year rather than only during filing season
  • Identify potential deductions, credits, and planning opportunities that may warrant further review
  • Better understand business-owner tax considerations when both business and personal information are available
  • Improve communication between tax preparation, tax planning, and financial advisory services
  • Reduce the likelihood of important information being overlooked
  • Create a more coordinated tax strategy throughout the year

More Than a Tax Return

The goal is not simply to prepare and file a tax return.

The goal is to integrate tax preparation into a larger financial strategy that supports tax planning, wealth management, business ownership, retirement planning, and long-term decision-making.

By maintaining an ongoing relationship with our clients throughout the year, Affinity Private Tax is able to provide a more coordinated approach than traditional tax-preparation firms that only engage during tax season.

Business Tax Returns

Business Tax Preparation: Accurate Returns Built on Clean Books

The business tax return is not just a compliance exercise. It is a direct reflection of the financial organization, bookkeeping accuracy, and tax planning that happened throughout the year.

At Affinity Private Tax, business tax returns are prepared as part of an integrated relationship—not as a standalone filing engagement.

Why Integration Matters

When your bookkeeper, tax planner, and tax preparer are part of the same coordinated team, the tax return benefits from cleaner data, fewer last-minute surprises, and a better understanding of the business’s full financial picture.

Because our team maintains your books throughout the year and coordinates tax planning before the return is prepared, the filing process is more organized, more accurate, and more strategically aligned with your long-term goals.

What Is Included

  • Business entity tax return preparation (S-Corp, C-Corp, Partnership, LLC, and Sole Proprietor)
  • Coordination with bookkeeping to ensure accurate, tax-ready financial records
  • Review of deductible expenses, depreciation, and business-related tax items
  • Coordination between business and owner-level individual returns
  • Communication with the IRS and state tax authorities when needed
  • Extension requests when appropriate
  • Year-round availability for tax questions and planning coordination

Prepared With Context

The biggest advantage of our approach is context. Because our team is involved in your business’s financial management throughout the year, we are not seeing your books for the first time in February or March.

This allows us to review your return with a broader understanding of your business activity, owner compensation decisions, entity structure, retirement contributions, and long-term tax strategy—rather than simply processing the documents provided at tax time.

The result is a more accurate return, fewer missed opportunities, and a smoother, less stressful filing experience for the business owner.

Business Tax Planning

Business Tax Planning

The Goal

The goal is not simply to minimize the tax bill at the end of the year. The goal is to make informed business decisions throughout the year—with their full tax impact considered in advance.

Why Our Approach Is Different

Most accounting relationships are compliance-focused. Tax planning, when it happens at all, often occurs after the fiscal year has ended—limiting the opportunities available.

At Affinity Private Tax, business tax planning is a year-round process. Because we maintain your books and have visibility into both business operations and owner-level finances, we can identify planning opportunities before decisions are made—not after.

What Is Included
  • Entity structure review and optimization
  • Owner compensation and distribution planning
  • Business deduction and depreciation strategies
  • Qualified Business Income (QBI) deduction planning
  • Retirement plan contributions and selection
  • Year-end tax estimates and projections
  • Coordination between business and owner individual tax planning
  • Planning around major business decisions (hires, equipment, real estate)

Planning Before Decisions Are Made

Business owners make decisions every day that carry tax consequences—compensation structures, capital purchases, timing of income and expenses, retirement contributions, and more.

Our goal is to be part of those conversations before decisions are finalized, not after. By coordinating business tax planning with bookkeeping, financial reporting, and the owner’s personal tax situation, we help ensure that major decisions are made with a full understanding of their tax impact.

“The best tax planning happens before the decision—not after the year-end.”
