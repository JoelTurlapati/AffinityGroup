Our Philosophy

Investing Built Around When You Need the Money — Not Your Age

Our fiduciary investment management approach is a time-tested, institutionalized process. We make every decision around a deep understanding of what the money is for, and exactly when it will be needed — not generic assumptions about your age or a risk-tolerance score on an intake form.

Philosophy

As a fiduciary advisor, we do not solicit investment products or hold preferred-provider relationships with any fund company. We never internally custody client investment assets — your portfolio is held at an independent, third-party custodian for your protection. We partner with the world's best-in-class money managers and have access to every investment vehicle under the sun, as a top fiduciary financial advisor should.

Process

For decades, our industry has leaned on a simplified, product-driven narrative: invest based on your age and a risk-tolerance quiz. But endowments, corporations, pension funds, and the wealthiest families in the world don't build portfolios that way — and neither do we.

We follow a detailed approach to understand the exact time horizon of every goal you're funding. Money needed in the next four to five years is held in low-volatility, capital-preservation investments. Money earmarked for goals further than five years out is invested in long-term growth vehicles such as equities, where time can work in your favor. This "bucketing" approach — segmenting capital by time horizon rather than by a single blended risk score — has been validated repeatedly in research by Nobel Prize–winning economists, and is the same framework used by university endowments and institutional investors.

“All investment should be made considering only one factor — when is the money needed.” — Warren Buffett
How We Build Your Portfolio

The Three-Bucket, Time-Horizon Strategy

Rather than one blended portfolio driven by a risk score, we segment your capital into three time horizons — the same framework used by endowments, pensions, and the wealthiest families in the country.

Bucket One
Short-Term Capital
0–4 Years

Money needed within the next four years is held in low-volatility, capital-preservation vehicles — cash equivalents, short-duration bonds, and CDs — so near-term goals are never exposed to market swings.

Bucket Two
Mid-Term Capital
4–5 Years

A blended allocation of conservative fixed income and modest equity exposure bridges the gap — balancing growth with stability as these goals approach.

Bucket Three
Long-Term Capital
5+ Years

Capital with a longer runway is invested for growth — primarily diversified equities — allowing time, compounding, and market growth to work in your favor.
