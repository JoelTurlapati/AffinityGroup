Fiduciary vs. Broker

Not all financial advisors are created equal. Understanding the difference between a fiduciary and a broker could be the most important financial decision you ever make.

Why the Fiduciary Difference Matters

When the person managing your money is legally bound to put your interests first, everything changes. As an independent fiduciary, every recommendation we make is built around your goals — never a commission or a quota.

Schedule a Consultation

What is a Fiduciary?

A fiduciary is a person or organization that acts on behalf of another person or persons, putting their clients’ interests ahead of their own, with a duty to preserve good faith and trust.

Broker / Registered Representative

Suitability Standard Only
  • Held only to a “suitability” standard — not “best interest”
  • May recommend products that earn them higher commissions
  • Not required to disclose all conflicts of interest
  • Can receive compensation from third-party product providers
  • No ongoing obligation after a transaction
  • May have proprietary product quotas or sales targets
Financial Advisors vs. Fiduciaries

Not All Financial Advisors Are Fiduciaries

Brokers, also known as registered representatives, are only held to a suitability standard, which requires that the broker make recommendations that are suitable based on a client’s personal situation — but the standard does not require the advice to be in the client’s best interest.

Therefore, some brokers may act for their own benefit. Being a fiduciary requires being bound both legally and ethically to act in the other’s best interests.
